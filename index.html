APARTMENT PLACE in Bountiful, UT

Welcome to Apartment Place, where being an apartment complex is what we’re doing. These buildings are fairly stationary, having concrete foundations and being too large for standard-size pockets. There is a tennis court and a pool, too, and your living box has an oven and some light fixtures. These living boxes feature some appliances, countertops, and some carpeting while also leaving enough space for you to sit with your things and maybe eat and have a conversation. We also have a gym (kinda) and some mailboxes and some covered parking. We have open parking, too, but there’s a bar across the street, and patrons frequently park illegally in resident parking. There’s a pool (some say it sparkles), a hot tub, a manager, and a maintenance man. We’re friendly to each other. Maybe we'll be friendly to you, too, if you’re cool, nomsayn?


I-15 North access is as convenient as it gets with the onramp just a couple hundred feet away. We're pretty close to downtown SLC, too (about 15 minutes), but far enough away to escape the busy-ness of the city. We take pictures sometimes while our tenants are out – you’ll find those below (we'll take them down for a small fee - $200 each.). You should come visit us because we’d like to profit from your poor life choices that have thus far prevented you from buying your own house.

Accessibility is one of those keywords that sounds like something you’d want when it comes to looking for a place to live. That's why we say things are accessible from here. We’re in the heart of Bountiful, but hopefully we won’t give it a heart attack. We don’t actually know if this area has a low crime rate (and the presence of Bountiful’s only bar a stone’s throw away makes that seem unlikely), but we can say that this area has a low arrest rate.


Pets are welcome here, and we do provide gloves and receptacles to help you clean up after your dog, so quit being a jerk and clean up after your dog. I don’t mind you having a dog – I like dogs. But it isn’t my dog, so please don’t make the grass into a minefield.


There are community grills available, but I’ve never seen anyone use them. But that’s cool because grills are allowed on your back deck, too. Just try to be cool with your neighbors. And while we’re on the subject, remember that you share walls with your neighbors. And we know how amazing that movie sounds with your new 7.2 surround sound system, but all we can hear down here is your subwoofer, so it’s hard to follow along with whatever explosion-filled action movie you’re falling asleep to this time. Please stop.


Anyway, we hope you’ll move in, pay your rent on time, pick up your stupid packages, and don’t complain too much. And leave us a nice review on the Google.
